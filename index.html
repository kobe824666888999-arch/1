The Real Challenges

Why you keep stopping —

声
01 · Tones

One wrong tone = a completely different word

Mā means mother. Mǎ means horse. Nobody corrects you in real life, so you keep making the same mistake for months without knowing.

"I practiced for months but kept saying 'horse' instead of 'mother.'"
速
02 · Speed

Real conversations move at 350 words per minute

You understand your teacher. The moment a real Chinese colleague or classmate replies naturally, your brain goes completely blank.

"The moment they replied fast, my mind went blank."
礼
03 · Culture

Technically correct but accidentally offensive

Chinese communication is about what's NOT said. Face (面子), indirect refusal, hierarchy in meetings — none of this is in any textbook.

"I was grammatically right but accidentally rude at a business dinner."
词
04 · Wrong Vocabulary

Apps teach words nobody at your job actually uses

Standard courses teach you "Manager Wang's office meeting." You need to negotiate a price, understand a contract clause, or survive a campus cafeteria.

"I can say 'the economic climate' but not 'the deadline was moved.'"
字
05 · Characters

No alphabet — 2,500 characters to read basic content

Every character looks like a painting. Most apps skip this, so you can speak but can't read a single WeChat message or sign on the street.

"I can say things out loud but can't read a menu."

Questions We Actually Get

I'm a complete beginner. Can I start?

Yes. Both courses start from zero — tones, pinyin, your first 150 characters. Everything is explained including the stuff other courses assume you already know. You don't need any Chinese background at all.

I'm arriving in 3 months. Is that enough time?

Yes — but you need to start now. The 1-on-1 bootcamp is specifically designed for people with a real deadline. 3 months of focused preparation is enough to arrive confident. Message me and we'll build a plan around your exact timeline.

How is this different from Duolingo or an app?

Apps optimize for streaks. This course optimizes for moments that matter — understanding your first business meeting, surviving campus orientation, catching what the supplier really said. You'll stick with it because what you're learning is directly relevant to your life in China.

I'm going for work — do I really need Chinese? Everyone speaks English, right?

The people who sign contracts and approve factory audits don't always speak English. The real conversations — the ones that decide whether you get the good price, the good terms, the real answer — happen in Chinese. You don't need to be fluent. You need enough to change the dynamic in the room.

How much time do I need per week?

30–40 minutes a day is enough if it's consistent. We'll help you build a realistic schedule around your actual life. Three focused sessions beats daily passive listening every time.

Can I study the business course even if I'm a student?

Absolutely. Many students in China end up doing internships, sourcing trips, or business projects. The business course teaches Chinese that's also useful for everyday professional and social situations — and it's a strong differentiator when you enter the job market.
