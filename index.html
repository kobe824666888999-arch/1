The Real Problems

Why you keep stopping — and it's not your fault.

"I work with Chinese people every day and get laughed at a lot because of my Chinese. Pls help." — DM received from a follower in Nigeria
01 · Tones

One wrong tone = a completely different word

Mā means mother. Mǎ means horse. Nobody corrects you in real life—so you keep making the same mistake for years without knowing it.

"I practiced for months but kept saying 'horse' instead of 'mother.'"
02 · Characters

No alphabet. 2,500 characters to read basic content.

Every character looks like a painting you have to memorize from scratch. Most apps skip this, so you can speak but can't read a single WeChat message.

"I can say things out loud but can't read a menu."
03 · Speed

Real conversations are 350 words per minute

You understand your teacher. You understand the drama with subtitles. The moment a native speaker replies naturally, your brain goes blank.

"The moment they replied, my mind went blank."
04 · Culture

Technically correct but accidentally rude

Chinese communication is about what's NOT said. Face (面子), indirect refusal, compliment culture—these rules aren't in any textbook.

"I was correct but accidentally rude at a business dinner."
05 · Vocabulary

Learning words nobody actually uses

Standard courses teach "Manager Wang's office meeting." You want to watch dramas, navigate 小红书, and talk about something you actually care about.

"I can explain economics in Chinese but not my skincare routine."
06 · Shame

Getting laughed at kills your progress faster than anything

Being corrected publicly. Making a tone mistake. Saying something embarrassing by accident. This shame spiral stops more learners than any grammar rule ever will.

"I knew the words. I just couldn't make them come out."

Questions We Actually Get

I'm a complete beginner. Can I start?

Yes. We start from absolute zero — pinyin, tones, your first 150 characters. We explain everything, including the stuff other courses assume you already know. You don't need any background at all.

I tried Duolingo for 6 months and stopped. How is this different?

Duolingo optimizes for streaks. We optimize for moments that matter — understanding your first drama line, surprising your partner, closing a deal. You'll stick with it because what you're learning is actually relevant to your life.

My goal is just to watch C-dramas. Is that valid?

100%. It's the #1 reason people join us. Start with the C-Drama path. Most students get their first subtitle-free moment within 8–10 weeks. It's a completely achievable and worthwhile goal.

I get embarrassed when I speak. Will this make it worse?

The opposite. Our AI Tone Coach gives you private, honest, non-judgmental feedback so you fix mistakes before you're ever in front of a real person. You'll feel ready before you speak out loud.

How much time do I need per week?

Three focused sessions of 30–40 minutes beats daily passive listening every time. We'll help you build a realistic schedule around your actual life, not some idealized version of it.

My reason is kind of embarrassing (a boyfriend / an idol / a drama)…

Those are the BEST reasons. Emotional motivation is the strongest fuel for language learning. We've seen people go from zero to conversational in months because they were learning for someone or something they genuinely loved. Bring your reason. We'll build around it.
